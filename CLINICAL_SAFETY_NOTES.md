---

## 1. Core Principle

RxDesk does NOT make clinical decisions.

It:
- captures
- structures
- flags
- prepares

Humans:
- review
- decide
- act

---

## 2. Key Risks

### Risk 1 — Incorrect medication captured
Mitigation:
- show transcript
- require human review
- editable fields

---

### Risk 2 — Urgent issue not flagged
Mitigation:
- keyword detection backup
- urgency indicators
- manual override

---

### Risk 3 — Over-trust in AI summary
Mitigation:
- label outputs as draft
- show confidence score
- show transcript

---

### Risk 4 — Wrong patient context
Mitigation:
- no automatic linking
- clear identifiers
- human confirmation

---

### Risk 5 — Missed follow-up
Mitigation:
- explicit workflow states
- persistent tracking
- dashboard visibility

---

## 3. Safety Rules

- No automatic dispensing decisions
- No automatic claim submission
- No silent task completion
- No hidden automation

---

## 4. Human-in-the-loop

Every task must:
- require review
- be editable
- be traceable
- show source data

---

## 5. Auditability

System must record:
- who reviewed
- what changed
- when it changed
- original vs edited values

---

## 6. Positioning

RxDesk is:

"A system that prepares and organises pharmacy workflow for staff review."

Not:
- autonomous
- a decision-maker
- a clinical system
